# The CEO Score and Weekly Review Process

> The CEO Score is Life OS Pro's weekly accountability mechanism. It converts the subjective feeling of "how was my week?" into a precise, repeatable 100-point measurement of alignment between your actions, your finances, and your goals.

## What Is the CEO Score?

The CEO Score is a 100-point weekly score calculated every Sunday during the Weekly Momentum Commander's Sunday Review. It measures three dimensions of performance:

| Dimension | Points | Question It Answers |
|---|---|---|
| Execution | 50 | Did you hit your Big Win this week? |
| Finance | 30 | Did you protect your budget integrity? |
| Mindset | 20 | Was a miss due to circumstance or avoidance? |

## The Three-Part Rubric

### Execution (50 points)
Measures whether you completed your single most important task — the "Big Win" — for the week.

- **Hit (50 pts)**: Big Win fully completed.
- **Partial (25 pts)**: Meaningful progress made but not fully completed.
- **Miss (0 pts)**: Big Win not attempted or abandoned.

The Big Win is not self-selected arbitrarily. It is derived from the Life Strategy Architect's quarterly roadmap, specifically from the active milestones in the current quarter. This ensures weekly execution connects directly to long-term goals.

### Finance (30 points)
Measures whether you avoided significant budget leaks during the week.

- **Secure (30 pts)**: No major unplanned spending. Safe-to-Spend respected.
- **Leak (15 pts)**: One or more budget categories exceeded, but damage was contained.
- **Critical Failure (0 pts)**: Major financial breach — significant overspend, ignored bills, or violated the Safety Buffer.

The Finance score pulls data from Tempo's weekly transaction summary. The Commander's Dual-Check Protocol requires both financial data AND goal progress before the score can be calculated.

### Mindset (20 points)
Measures the quality of your self-awareness when a miss occurs.

- **Valid reason (20 pts)**: Miss attributed to a legitimate external factor — illness, family emergency, genuine crisis. The system accepts the miss and moves on.
- **Avoidance (0 pts)**: Miss attributed to procrastination, fear, distraction, or comfort-seeking. The system flags this pattern and includes it in the recalibration plan.

The Mindset dimension exists because two people can both score 0 on Execution but have fundamentally different situations. Illness is not the same as avoidance, and the system distinguishes between them.

## Score Tier Labels

| Score Range | Color | Label |
|---|---|---|
| 80–100 | Green | EXCELLENT ALIGNMENT |
| 50–79 | Amber | DRIFT DETECTED |
| 0–49 | Red | CRITICAL MISALIGNMENT |

**EXCELLENT ALIGNMENT (80–100):** Your actions, money, and goals are in sync. The system confirms the current approach and builds the next week's Flight Plan as a continuation.

**DRIFT DETECTED (50–79):** Partial alignment. The system identifies which dimension is lagging, diagnoses whether it is a strategy failure (bad plan) or execution failure (inconsistent habits), and adjusts the next week's priorities accordingly.

**CRITICAL MISALIGNMENT (0–49):** Significant disconnect between stated values and actual behavior. The system initiates a recalibration — revisiting the Architect's quarterly roadmap, checking for overcommitment, and generating a reduced-scope Flight Plan to rebuild momentum.

## The Weekly Flight Plan

The CEO Score is not just a grade — it is the input to the Weekly Flight Plan, which is the Commander's primary output. Every Sunday Review concludes with a Flight Plan containing:

- **Theme**: One word that defines the focus and energy of the coming week (e.g., "Momentum," "Recovery," "Launch").
- **The Big 3**: Three non-negotiable tasks derived from the Architect's quarterly roadmap. These are the minimum viable deliverables for the week.
- **Financial Guardrail**: One specific, actionable spending rule based on the leaks identified in the Finance score. Example: "No restaurant spending this week — pack lunch and use the $40 saved toward the emergency fund."
- **CEO Score**: The final calculated score with a one-sentence plain-language explanation.

## Operational Modes

### The Sunday Review
The core weekly ritual. Runs retrospectively (what happened last week?) and prospectively (what is the plan for next week?). Calculates the CEO Score and generates the Flight Plan.

### The Friday Forecast
An optional mid-week pre-mortem. The Commander reviews the current week's trajectory, identifies weekend financial risks (social events, impulse spending triggers, planned purchases), and offers scripts and tactics for navigating high-risk moments without blowing the budget or abandoning the Big Win.

## The Dual-Check Protocol

A hard-coded rule in the Commander: if a user provides only financial data (bank transactions, Tempo summary), the AI halts and explicitly asks for progress on the Life Architect goals before proceeding. Both data streams — money AND goal execution — are required inputs for a valid CEO Score. This prevents the common trap of only tracking finances while ignoring whether the week actually moved the needle on long-term priorities.
