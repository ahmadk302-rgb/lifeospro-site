# Module 1: Life Strategy Architect

## Purpose
The Life Strategy Architect is an AI-powered executive coach that guides users through designing a holistic, multi-year life plan. It translates vague ambitions into measurable, actionable quarterly roadmaps.

## The Dual Track System
All planning is organized into two parallel tracks:
- **Professional Track**: Career, business, and financial goals.
- **Personal Track**: Family, health, identity, and lifestyle goals.

This separation creates clarity and enables the system to detect conflicts between tracks.

## The 4-Quadrant Matrix
Within each track, goals are categorized into four quadrants:
- **DO (Blue)**: Experiences, achievements, and projects you want to complete. These are output-oriented goals. Examples: Launch a SaaS MVP, Run a marathon.
- **HAVE (Indigo)**: Assets, money, resources, and tools you want to acquire. These are input-oriented goals. Examples: Achieve $10K MRR, Set up a home gym.
- **BE (Purple)**: Identity, character traits, and skills you want to develop. These are internal growth goals. Examples: Become a thought leader, Be a patient father.
- **LIVE (Emerald)**: Routines, boundaries, and environment that make up your ideal lifestyle. These are lifestyle design goals. Examples: No meetings on Fridays, Eat dinner at home 5 nights per week.

## The Prime Directive
The system's golden rule: Always protect the Personal LIVE quadrant from being overwhelmed by Professional DO ambition. When the system detects a conflict (such as a professional goal to "Travel 200 days for work" conflicting with a personal goal to "Be home for dinner 5 times per week"), it flags this as a "Sustainability Risk" and forces the user to confront the trade-off.

## Operating Modes
- **Mode A (Architect)**: New Year Setup. Collects Professional and Personal quadrant inputs, clarifies vague goals, identifies conflicts and synergies, generates a quarterly roadmap, proposes one "One Big Habit" per quarter.
- **Mode B (Accountability)**: Check-In. Reviews wins, misses, and blockers. Diagnoses misses as Strategy Failure (flawed plan) or Execution Failure (inconsistent habits). Provides recalibration plan.
- **Mode C (Annual Rollover)**: Legacy Mode. Reviews unfinished goals, labels items as Carry Forward, Compound, or Drop. Identifies repeating patterns and builds safeguards.

## Smart Rollover Logic
When planning a new year, BE (identity) and LIVE (lifestyle) quadrants carry forward automatically, preserving core identity and standards. DO (achievements) and HAVE (assets) reset to empty, providing a clean slate.

## Key Output
Quarterly Roadmap: A visual grid showing Q1 through Q4, each with a highlighted "One Big Habit" in amber, plus Professional Focus and Personal Focus checklists with interactive checkboxes.
