Questions

Frequently asked.

How is this different from YNAB?

YNAB is great at envelope budgeting, but it doesn't know about your goals, your projects, or your weekly execution. Life OS connects your spending to your actual life vision through the Shared Memory architecture. You also get phase-based budgeting aligned to your paychecks, credit card float protection, and a weekly accountability system that YNAB simply doesn't offer.

How is this different from Monarch?

Monarch is excellent at showing you what happened. Life OS tells you what to do next. Monarch is a rearview mirror. Life OS is a windshield. While Monarch excels at aggregation and net worth tracking, it doesn't drive behavior change through weekly reviews, execution coaching, or connecting your spending to your life goals.

Do I have to enter transactions manually?

You can do both. Connect your bank via Plaid and transactions auto-import with smart category matching. You can also log transactions manually — both sources live side-by-side, so nothing gets double-counted. Manual entries are great for cash spending or splitting purchases. The best of both worlds: automatic data with intentional control.

What's the "phase system" and why does it matter?

Most budgeting apps treat the month as one block. But you don't get paid once a month, you get paid every two weeks. The phase system splits your month into Phase 1 (days 1-15) and Phase 2 (days 16-end). This prevents the "false confidence" of seeing a full bank account when rent hasn't hit yet, and catches liquidity traps before they happen.

Can I try it before paying?

Yes. Founding members get a free trial period to explore the system. We want you to feel the difference before you commit. Once you're in, the $9.99/month founding price is locked for life and will never increase.

What happens to my data?

Your data is yours. We use Supabase for secure, encrypted storage with row-level security, meaning no one (not even other users) can access your information. We don't sell data, don't show ads, and don't share your financial information with third parties. Period.

What if I already use Notion for goal tracking?

Notion is powerful for notes and project management, but it can't touch your money. Life OS replaces the goal-tracking part of Notion with something that's directly wired to your budget and weekly execution. Your goals stop being aspirational text and start driving real financial and behavioral decisions.

Is this just for people who are bad with money?

Not at all. Life OS is built for power users who are already disciplined but frustrated that their tools don't connect. If you've outgrown spreadsheets, feel limited by YNAB's approach, or want your financial decisions to actually align with your life goals, this is for you. It's a system for people who want to be intentional, not just organized.

How is the Debt Freedom Engine different from other debt apps?

Every debt app shows you a payoff timeline in isolation. Life OS is the only system where paying off a debt actually changes your budget. When you add a debt, its minimum payment automatically becomes a bill in Tempo. When you pay it off, that money frees up your Safe-to-Spend immediately. Commander celebrates the win in your weekly review. Architect links debt payoff to your HAVE goals. It's not a separate tracker — it's integrated into how your entire financial life operates.
